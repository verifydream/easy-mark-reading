### **1. Executive Summary**

*   A brief overview of the project, its main goal, and expected impact.

---

### **2. Problem Statement / Opportunity**

*   Clearly articulate the problem this project aims to solve for users or the business, or the opportunity it seeks to capture.
*   *Example:* "Users currently struggle with discoverability of relevant content, leading to frustration and lower engagement. This project aims to centralize content and provide personalized recommendations."

---

### **3. Project Goals & Objectives**

*   **Overall Goal:** The overarching strategic aim.
*   **SMART Objectives:** Specific, Measurable, Achievable, Relevant, Time-bound objectives.
    *   *Example:* "Increase user engagement time by 15% within 3 months of launch."
    *   *Example:* "Reduce customer support tickets related to content discovery by 20%."

---

### **4. Target Audience**

*   Who are we building this for? (e.g., "Existing premium subscribers who log in daily," "New users during their onboarding week").
*   Include relevant demographics or user behaviors.

---

### **5. Key Features & Functionality (High-Level)**

*   A bulleted list of the core features that will be delivered.
    *   *Example:*
        *   Personalized content recommendation engine.
        *   Categorized content browsing.
        *   User-generated content submission portal.
        *   Improved search functionality.

---

### **6. User Stories (Optional / Detailed in full PRD)**

*   Brief descriptions of how users will interact with the new features.
    *   *Example:*
        *   **As a** frequent reader, **I want to** see recommendations based on my past reading history, **so that I can** easily find more content I'll enjoy.
        *   **As an** administrator, **I want to** approve user-submitted content, **so that I can** maintain content quality.

---

### **7. Out of Scope**

*   What will *not* be part of this initial project release?
    *   *Example:* "Advanced analytics dashboards for content performance will be considered in a future phase."

---

### **8. Success Metrics (KPIs)**

*   How will we measure the project's success against the objectives?
    *   *Example:*
        *   Average session duration.
        *   Click-through rate on recommended content.
        *   Number of support tickets related to content.
        *   Net Promoter Score (NPS) improvement.

---

### **9. Dependencies & Assumptions**

*   **Dependencies:** What other projects, teams, or resources are we relying on?
    *   *Example:* "Requires integration with existing user authentication service."
*   **Assumptions:** What are we taking as given for this project?
    *   *Example:* "Assume existing data infrastructure can support new content volumes."

---

### **10. Proposed Timeline / Phases (High-Level)**

*   A rough outline of major milestones or phases.
    *   *Example:*
        *   **Phase 1 (Discovery & Design):** Weeks 1-4
        *   **Phase 2 (Development - Core Features):** Weeks 5-12
        *   **Phase 3 (Testing & Refinement):** Weeks 13-15
        *   **Launch:** Week 16

---

### **11. Key Stakeholders**

*   Who needs to be informed, consulted, or involved in decisions?
    *   *Example:* Product Leadership, Engineering Lead, Marketing Team, Customer Support.

---

### **12. Potential Risks & Mitigations**

*   What could go wrong, and how might we address it?
    *   *Example:*
        *   **Risk:** Scope creep during development.
        *   **Mitigation:** Maintain strict feature prioritization and hold regular scope review meetings.

---

### **13. Technical Considerations (Briefly)**

*   Any critical technical details or architectural decisions.
    *   *Example:* "Will be built on our existing microservices architecture, leveraging AWS Lambda for the recommendation engine."
